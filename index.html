The Safest Private Airplane Ever Built

The Cessna 172 is one of the safest, best built small airplanes ever. Thousands of 172's built
in the 1960's are still operating today, particularly as trainers in flight schools. When first
introduced they were relatively cheap and extremely safe. In fact, to make the plane
stall requires considerable effort. It really "wants" to fly even when the pilot makes a mistake.

The Cessna 172 is easy to fly. While all flying takes months of studying and practice to
learn and get your license, the 172 is one of the easiest and safest. Landing an airplane
is one the most difficult tasks to master. However, the 172 makes it look easy. Click on the
link below to view a brief video showing a 172 making a nearly perfect landing.

Cessna 172 Making Nearly Perfect Landing

It is relatively expensive to learn how to fly. The cost ranges from about $10,000 to $15,000.
It depends on how long it takes the student to learn to fly. Federal regulations require that you
have at least 40 hours of flight training and 10 hours of ground school to get your pilot's license.
The majority of that cost is airplane rental at $150 - $200 per hour. The Cessna 172 is one of the
cheapest planes to rent so using one will keep the cost of learning to fly down to a minimum.
